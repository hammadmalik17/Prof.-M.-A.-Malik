About

Scholar. Teacher.
Public Intellectual.

A committed economist and prolific scholar, Prof. Malik has spent over two decades at the intersection of academic rigour and real-world policy—teaching undergraduate, postgraduate, and intermediate students, and writing for wider public audiences.

Rooted in Osmania University's intellectual tradition, his research spans agricultural economics, inclusive development, minority empowerment, and Telangana's regional political economy. His column in Andhra Jyothi—running for 24 years—brings economic analysis to Telugu-speaking communities across the state.

Beyond the classroom, he serves as a community organiser and civic leader, founding several educational and social advocacy organisations.

Economics for the Public: A 24-Year Column

For over two decades, Prof. Malik has contributed regular columns on Economics to Andhra Jyothi—one of Telugu's most widely read daily newspapers. His writing demystifies complex economic policy, explains the impact of national schemes on Telangana's communities, and brings academic analysis to general readership.

This sustained commitment to public scholarship reflects a belief that economics should not remain confined to the lecture hall—it should inform, empower, and engage citizens in the democratic conversation about their economic futures.
